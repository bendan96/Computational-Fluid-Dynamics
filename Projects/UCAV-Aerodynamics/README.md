# UCAV Aerodynamics Investigation

**Experimental and Numerical Study of Vortex-Dominated Flow on a Highly Swept Unmanned Combat Aerial Vehicle**

---

## 📌 Project Overview

This project investigates the nonlinear aerodynamic characteristics of a highly swept UCAV using a combination of wind tunnel experiments and CFD simulations in ANSYS Fluent.

**Focus Areas**: Leading-edge vortex (LEV) formation, vortex lift, vortex breakdown, and post-stall behavior.

---

## 📊 Key Findings

- Strong vortex lift enhancement up to approximately 20° angle of attack.
- Vortex breakdown begins beyond 22°, leading to stall and increased induced drag.
- Good agreement between experimental and numerical results in trend up to moderate AoA.
- Discrepancies at high AoA mainly due to limitations of steady RANS (k-ω SST) model.

---

## 🔬 Discussion

[→ Full Discussion Here](./summary/discussion.md)

Key highlights from the discussion:
- The formation of primary and secondary vortices significantly delays stall.
- CFD captures vortex structures well at low-to-moderate AoA but underpredicts peak lift at high incidence.
- Reynolds number effects and transitional flow play an important role in the observed differences.
- The configuration demonstrates good longitudinal stability (nose-down pitching moment).
