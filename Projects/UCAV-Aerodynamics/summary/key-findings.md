# Key Findings

### Aerodynamic Performance
- **Vortex Lift**: Strong leading-edge vortices form at moderate angles of attack, significantly enhancing lift up to ~20°.
- **Stall Behavior**: Vortex breakdown begins beyond 22°, leading to a reduction in lift curve slope and increased drag.
- **Maximum Lift**: Experimental Cl reached 1.39 at 28°, while CFD predicted 1.155 at 30°.

### Experimental vs Numerical Comparison
- Good overall agreement in lift, drag, and pitching moment trends up to moderate AoA.
- Mean Absolute Error for lift coefficient (Cl) ≈ 14% in the linear and vortex-lift regions.
- CFD tends to underpredict lift at high AoA and slightly overpredict drag at low AoA.

### Flow Physics
- Formation of primary and secondary vortices observed.
- Vortex cores strengthen with increasing AoA up to 20°, after which breakdown migrates upstream.
- The configuration exhibits natural longitudinal stability (negative pitching moment slope).

### Practical Implications
- Vortex-dominated flow significantly improves post-stall maneuverability for UCAVs.
- Substantial increase in induced drag accompanies high-angle vortex lift.
- Steady RANS (k-ω SST) is adequate for preliminary design but limited for high-AoA predictions.
