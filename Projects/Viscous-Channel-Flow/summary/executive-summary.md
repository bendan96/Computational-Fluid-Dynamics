# Executive Summary

This project presents a comprehensive **Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD)** analysis of laminar viscous flow through a two-dimensional channel using ANSYS Fluent. The study serves as a classic benchmark problem due to the existence of an exact analytical solution (Poiseuille flow).

### Objectives
- Simulate fully developed laminar channel flow at Re = 100.
- Conduct systematic **mesh independence studies** on both full and half-domain geometries.
- Validate numerical results against the analytical parabolic velocity profile.
- Investigate the benefits of symmetry boundary conditions and explore advanced CFD concepts.

### Key Outcomes
- Excellent agreement between CFD and analytical solution, with centreline velocity reaching **1.500 m/s** (exact value = 1.5 m/s).
- Mesh independence successfully demonstrated: fine mesh (full domain) and even coarse mesh (half domain) yielded highly accurate results.
- Half-domain symmetry model provided comparable accuracy with significantly reduced computational cost.
- The study reinforces best practices in CFD verification, validation, and numerical setup for internal flows.

This work strengthens foundational understanding of laminar internal flow simulation, mesh convergence, and the effective use of symmetry in CFD modelling.
